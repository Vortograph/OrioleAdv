Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

  • What does it mean to be an Oriole Advocate?
    Oriole Advocates are people who dedicate themselves to lending a voice to the students, teachers, and staff of the Avon Community School Corporation to promote student achievement and community engagement.
  • What kind of person makes a good Advocate?
    A good Advocate is any parent, caregiver, community member, or concerned adult who wants to make a positive and constructive impact. We are a diverse group united around the shared vision of helping ACSC continue to be one of the premier school corporations in Indiana.
  • What topics will I learn about as an Advocate?
    Along with plenty of time to build relationships among Advocates, monthly meetings are planned around topics that include: school legislation, finance, growth plans, tech, food service, school safety, mental health, curriculum, and more.

    You will also get to hear from our corporation teachers of the year, interact with members of the Board of School Trustees, share coffee with Legislators, tour schools, and much more.
  • What does it mean to be an Oriole Advocate?
    The Oriole Advocates meet once a month during the school year. Meetings typically last between 90 minutes and two hours. Meetings are structured in an academy format and classes are interconnected.
